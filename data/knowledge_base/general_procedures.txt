DOCUMENT: General Crane Maintenance Procedures and Safety
DOCUMENT TYPE: General Reference Manual
APPLIES TO: All Overhead Cranes

=== LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE ===
MANDATORY before any maintenance work on crane:

1. Notify all affected personnel
2. Identify all energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, stored mechanical)
3. Switch off main isolator on crane panel
4. Apply personal padlock to isolator
5. Attach danger tag with name, date, reason
6. Test: attempt to start crane (pendant or radio) → must not respond
7. Verify voltage at motor terminals: 0V on all phases
8. Commence maintenance work
9. After work: remove tools and personnel, then remove lock/tag
10. Document in maintenance log

Multiple workers: EACH worker applies their own lock (multi-lock hasp)

=== PRE-SHIFT INSPECTION CHECKLIST ===
Operator must check before first use of shift:
□ Wire rope: visible damage, kinks, correct reeving
□ Hook: throat opening within tolerance, safety latch functions
□ Pendant/remote: all buttons function correctly, cable not damaged
□ Upper limit switch: test by raising hook until limit activates
□ Brakes: test stop function (short travel with no load)
□ Structure: visible cracks, deformed members, loose bolts
□ Rail: clear of obstruction, no visible damage
□ Load test: no-load run in all axes before lifting

=== LOAD TESTING REQUIREMENTS ===
- After major repair or component replacement: 100% load test minimum
- After structural repair: 110% overload test with static hold (10 minutes)
- Brake testing: 150% static hold after any brake work
- Annual inspection: 100% load test required by EN 13001 and national standards
- New installation: full proof load test per design specification

=== ENVIRONMENTAL OPERATING LIMITS ===
- Temperature:
  - Standard motors: -20°C to +40°C (derate above 40°C)
  - Cold store cranes: special low-temp lubrication and heaters required
  - High temp (foundry): special motor class, refractory coatings
- Humidity: >90% RH → enhanced corrosion protection, IP65+ required
- Dust: Class M or H environments → sealed motors, filtered panels, regular cleaning
- Explosive atmosphere: ATEX-rated components required (do not use standard equipment)

=== FAULT DOCUMENTATION STANDARD ===
Each fault/maintenance event should record:
1. Date and time
2. Crane ID and location
3. Symptom (observed behaviour)
4. Measurements taken (voltage, current, temperature, etc.)
5. Root cause identified
6. Action taken (repair, replacement, adjustment)
7. Parts replaced (part number, serial if known)
8. Post-repair test result
9. Technician name and signature
10. Next action required (if any)

=== COMPETENCY REQUIREMENTS ===
- Routine inspection: Trained crane operator
- Electrical testing: Qualified electrician (country-specific certification)
- Load testing after repair: Competent person (crane engineer or appointed inspector)
- Structural inspection: Qualified crane engineer
- NDT (magnetic particle, dye-penetrant): Certified NDT technician

=== COMMON CRANE ERROR CODES (GENERIC PLC) ===
E01 – Overload relay tripped (motor overcurrent)
E02 – Phase loss detected
E03 – Emergency stop activated
E04 – Upper hoist limit reached
E05 – Lower hoist limit reached
E06 – Bridge end limit (left)
E07 – Bridge end limit (right)
E08 – Trolley end limit
E09 – Motor thermal trip (PTC thermistor)
E10 – Control voltage loss
E11 – Anti-collision sensor activated
E12 – Overload protection (load cell >100% rated)
E13 – Communication fault (radio remote or PLC)
E14 – Brake release fault (brake current feedback missing)

=== SPARE PARTS MANAGEMENT ===
Critical spares to maintain on-site:
- Brake coil assembly (matched to installed brake)
- Brake disc and friction pads
- Contactor set (matching current rating)
- Overload relay
- Control fuses (all ratings used)
- Wire rope (1 drum length, matching specification)
- Limit switches (matching types installed)
- Rectifier module for brake supply
- Motor bearings (matched to installed motors)
- Oil (correct grade for gearboxes)

=== KNOWLEDGE UPDATE (2026-04-27 — added by KE: Demo KE) ===
Safety-critical maintenance procedures for wire rope systems must be carried out exclusively by competent personnel with appropriate training and experience, in accordance with standards such as EN 13001 and applicable local regulations. In cases of suspected rope damage, a comprehensive inspection is required, including measurement of wire breaks, evaluation of diameter reduction, and examination of the entire rope length, including drum wraps and termination points. Additionally, associated components such as drum grooves, sheaves, and rope anchor points must be inspected for any secondary damage. If defects are confirmed, rope replacement must be performed using the correct specification, ensuring proper installation and pre-load commissioning. Finally, a post-installation inspection and formal sign-off are mandatory before the crane can be safely returned to service.
