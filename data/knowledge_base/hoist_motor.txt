COMPONENT: Hoist Motor
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: Demag EKKE 5t, Liebherr Tower Crane, Generic Overhead Crane

=== OVERVIEW ===
The hoist motor drives the lifting drum to raise and lower loads. It is typically a three-phase asynchronous motor with integrated thermal protection. Proper voltage supply, adequate cooling, and mechanical alignment are critical for reliable operation.

=== ELECTRICAL SPECIFICATIONS ===
- Rated voltage: 380-415V AC (3-phase, 50 Hz) or 460-480V AC (60 Hz variants)
- Insulation class: F (155°C) or H (180°C)
- Protection class: IP55 minimum (dusty/outdoor environments: IP65)
- Starting current: 5–7× rated full-load current (FLA)
- Duty cycle: S3 (intermittent) – typically 40% or 60% CDF (cyclic duration factor)
- Locked rotor current: ≤ 600% of FLA

NAMEPLATE VALUES TO CHECK:
- FLA (Full Load Amps): Compare running current; >110% FLA indicates overload
- Power factor (cosφ): Typical 0.7–0.85 at full load
- Insulation resistance: Minimum 1 MΩ at 500V DC (new: >100 MΩ)

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. MOTOR FAILS TO START
   Causes:
   - Loss of one phase (single-phasing) → motor hums, overheats quickly
   - Overload relay tripped
   - Control fuse blown
   - Motor thermistor (PTC) triggered due to overtemperature
   - Contactor contact failure
   - Brake not releasing (mechanical lock)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Check supply voltage on all 3 phases at motor terminals (should be within ±5% of rated)
   b. Check overload relay status – reset if tripped; investigate root cause before restart
   c. Verify contactor closes fully (check coil voltage: typically 24VDC or 230VAC)
   d. Measure motor winding resistance (should be balanced ±5% between phases)
   e. Check motor thermistor continuity (PTC open = thermistor triggered)

2. MOTOR OVERHEATING (Thermal trip, body >90°C)
   Causes:
   - Overload (excessive load, stuck mechanism)
   - Ambient temperature >40°C without derating
   - Blocked cooling fan or dirty fins
   - Voltage imbalance >2% between phases
   - Motor running in wrong direction (phase reversal)
   - Excessive duty cycle beyond rated CDF
   - Bearing failure causing increased friction

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Measure winding temperature using IR thermometer on motor housing
   b. Check voltage balance: Imbalance% = (Max deviation from average / Average) × 100
   c. Verify cooling fan rotation and fin cleanliness
   d. Check ambient temperature and ventilation
   e. Measure running current vs. nameplate FLA on all phases

   Thermal limits:
   - Winding temperature rise: max 80K above ambient (Class F)
   - Motor housing surface: typically <90°C (touch test: hold 5 seconds max)

3. ABNORMAL VIBRATION / NOISE
   Causes:
   - Bearing damage (grinding/rumbling noise, high vibration)
   - Rotor imbalance (continuous hum with cyclic vibration)
   - Loose mounting bolts
   - Coupling misalignment
   - Debris ingress into motor

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Measure vibration amplitude at bearing housings (normal: <2.8 mm/s RMS per ISO 10816)
   b. Check coupling alignment: radial and angular misalignment <0.1 mm
   c. Inspect motor mounting bolts for tightness
   d. Listen for change in noise pitch with load variation

4. MOTOR RUNS BUT LOAD DOES NOT LIFT
   Causes:
   - Brake not releasing
   - Gearbox failure
   - Coupling shear pin failure
   - Drum shaft key failure

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter               | Normal Range          | Action Threshold
------------------------|----------------------|------------------
Supply Voltage (3-ph)   | 380–415V (±5%)       | <361V or >436V → Stop
Phase Imbalance         | <2%                  | >3% → Investigate immediately
Running Current         | ≤100% FLA            | >110% FLA → Reduce load
Insulation Resistance   | >1 MΩ (500V DC test) | <1 MΩ → Do not energise
Winding Resistance      | Balanced ±5%         | >10% imbalance → Rewinding needed
Vibration (bearing)     | <2.8 mm/s RMS        | >4.5 mm/s RMS → Replace bearing
Motor Housing Temp      | <70°C                | >90°C → Stop immediately

=== MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE ===
- Weekly: Visual inspection, check for unusual noise
- Monthly: Check mounting bolts, clean cooling fins
- 6-Monthly: Grease bearings (if re-greasing type), check coupling
- Annual: Full electrical check (insulation resistance, winding balance), bearing inspection
- 5-Year: Full motor rewind assessment

=== DEMAG EKKE 5t SPECIFIC NOTES ===
- Motor type: KBF-series asynchronous motor with integrated brake
- Rated power: 1.5–5.5 kW depending on variant
- Brake coil supply: 24VDC (rectified from 230VAC)
- Thermistor type: PTC (100-110°C trip point)
- Bearing type: 6205/6206 deep groove (both ends)
- Standard duty: FEM 2m (S3-25%)




=== KNOWLEDGE UPDATE (2026-06-17 — added by KE: Demo KE) ===
OVERLOAD RELAY DERATING — HIGH AMBIENT TEMPERATURE

When the workshop or installation ambient temperature exceeds 20°C, the overload relay trip current must be reduced (derated) to ensure correct thermal protection.

Standard derating rule:
- For every 1°C above 20°C ambient, reduce the relay setting by approximately 1.5% to 2%.
- Example: Motor rated at 15A in 20°C ambient. Workshop temperature is 38°C (18°C above reference). Derating = 18 x 1.75% = 31.5%. Adjusted setting = 15A x (1 - 0.315) = approximately 10.3A.

Inspection and adjustment procedure:
1. Isolate the panel (turn off main isolator and lock out).
2. Locate the overload relay — labelled OL1 or OL2 on the panel diagram.
3. Read the current setting on the relay dial (usually on the front face of the relay).
4. Check the relay manufacturer datasheet (usually stored inside the panel door) for the derating table.
5. Calculate the correct setting using the ambient temperature at the installation site.
6. Adjust the relay dial to the calculated value.
7. Record the new setting, date, and technician name in the maintenance log.
8. Re-energise the panel and test motor operation under normal load.
