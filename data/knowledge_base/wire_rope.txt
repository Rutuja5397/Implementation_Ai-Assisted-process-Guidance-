COMPONENT: Wire Rope
DOCUMENT TYPE: Inspection and Replacement Manual
APPLIES TO: Overhead Cranes, Tower Cranes, All Wire Rope Hoist Systems

=== OVERVIEW ===
Wire rope is a safety-critical load-bearing component. Regular inspection per EN 13001-3-2, ISO 4309, and ASME B30.2 is mandatory. Wire rope must be discarded before reaching discard criteria to prevent catastrophic failure.

=== CONSTRUCTION TYPES ===
- 6×19 (6 strands, 19 wires per strand) – General hoist use
- 6×36 (6 strands, 36 wires per strand) – Better fatigue resistance, more flexible
- 8×19, 8×36 – Multi-layer drum applications
- Rotation-resistant (18×7, 19×7) – Tower cranes, single fall reeving

=== INSPECTION CRITERIA (Per ISO 4309) ===

DISCARD CRITERIA – MANDATORY REPLACEMENT if ANY of the following:

1. BROKEN WIRES
   - 6×19 rope: 6 broken wires in 1 rope lay length, or 3 in one strand
   - 6×36 rope: 14 broken wires in 1 rope lay length, or 7 in one strand
   - Any broken wire near end fitting: immediate discard
   - Fatigue breaks (square ends): more serious than abrasive breaks (tapered ends)

2. DIAMETER REDUCTION
   - Nominal diameter reduction >5% (measured with calibrated calipers)
   - Example: 10 mm rope → discard at ≤9.5 mm
   - Check at multiple points (drum contact zones, sheave contact zones)
   - Internal corrosion may cause diameter reduction before visual wire breaks appear

3. KINKING / DEFORMATION
   - Any visible kink (permanent bend) → immediate discard
   - Bird-caging (outer strands separated from core) → immediate discard
   - Core protrusion → immediate discard
   - Crushing deformation

4. CORROSION
   - Internal corrosion (rattling sound when rope bent; stiff, reduced diameter) → discard
   - Surface rust: light surface rust acceptable if wire surface unaffected
   - Pitting corrosion on individual wires → discard

5. HEAT DAMAGE
   - Any evidence of arc burns, welding spatter, or heat discolouration → discard
   - Rope that has been in fire → discard

=== INSPECTION PROCEDURE ===
Frequency:
- Before each shift: visual check of accessible length
- Monthly: detailed inspection of full rope length
- Annually: independent inspection by competent person
- After shock loading or incident: full inspection immediately

Procedure:
1. Unspool rope from drum (if possible) or traverse hook to expose all sections
2. Clean rope with wire brush (do not use solvents that strip lubrication)
3. Inspect under good lighting; use magnifying glass for wire breaks
4. Measure diameter at: drum first wrap, last wrap, and at each sheave contact point
5. Flex rope by hand to check for stiffness (indicates core corrosion)
6. Record all findings on inspection form with date, length markers, and photographs

=== LUBRICATION ===
- Rope should be lubricated per manufacturer schedule (typically 3–6 months)
- Use penetrating rope lubricant (not heavy grease that blocks internal penetration)
- Lubricant should penetrate to core
- Over-lubrication: excessive buildup attracts dirt → abrasive wear
- Dry rope: accelerated fatigue and corrosion

=== ROPE INSTALLATION ===
- Direction of lay must match drum winding direction
- Minimum 3 dead turns on drum at lowest hook position
- Rope end termination: swaged socket (factory), wedge socket, or wire rope clip
- Wire rope clips: minimum 3 clips, correct orientation (U-bolt on dead end), torque to spec
- After installation: load test at 10% capacity, inspect, then full capacity test

=== SHEAVE INSPECTION ===
- Sheave groove radius = 0.53–0.55 × rope diameter (too small = pinching; too large = rope distortion)
- Check for groove wear (use sheave gauge template)
- Sheave bearing: check for rough rotation, side play

=== HOOK BLOCK WEIGHT AND REEVING ===
- Single fall (1 rope): maximum for small hoists <2t
- Double fall (2 ropes): standard for 2–10t
- Multiple fall: larger capacities

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter              | Acceptable            | Discard Threshold
-----------------------|-----------------------|------------------
Diameter (vs nominal)  | ≥95% of nominal       | <95% of nominal
Wire breaks (6×19)     | 0–5 per lay length    | ≥6 per lay length
Wire breaks (6×36)     | 0–13 per lay length   | ≥14 per lay length
Kink / deformation     | None                  | Any visible kink
Surface corrosion      | Light surface rust    | Pitting or internal corrosion
Rope stiffness         | Flexible throughout   | Rigid sections → internal damage
End fitting integrity  | No cracks, full seating| Any crack or partial seating

=== RECORD KEEPING ===
Each wire rope must have an individual record:
- Installation date
- Rope type, diameter, length, and grade
- Drum and sheave details
- All inspection dates and findings
- Discard date and reason
