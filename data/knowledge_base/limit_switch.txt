COMPONENT: Limit Switch (Hoist Upper/Lower, Travel End-of-Travel)
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: All Overhead Crane Types

=== OVERVIEW ===
Limit switches are safety devices that stop crane motion before mechanical end-stops are reached. The hoist upper limit switch prevents over-travel of the hook block into the hoist machinery. Travel limit switches prevent bridge or trolley from running off the end of the runway/rail. All limit switches are wired in the normally-closed (NC) safety circuit – if they fail, the circuit opens and motion stops.

=== TYPES ===
1. Rotary/Cam-operated limit switch (upper/lower hoist limits via rope drum rotation counter)
2. Roller-arm mechanical limit switch (direct contact by hook block or travelling assembly)
3. Inductive proximity sensor (non-contact; more reliable in dirty environments)
4. Rope/slack detection switch (separate; detects slack rope condition)

=== HOIST UPPER LIMIT SWITCH ===
Critical safety device: prevents hook block from over-travelling into hoist machinery.
- Must stop hoist motion in UP direction with minimum 300mm clearance to hoist body
- After triggering: only DOWN motion should be permitted
- Test frequency: before each shift (mandatory per EN 14502)

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. HOIST WILL NOT TRAVEL UP (Limit switch falsely activated)
   Causes:
   - Limit switch arm bent or displaced (bumped out of position)
   - Cam/actuator worn (rotary type – not returning to correct position)
   - Contacts corroded/stuck open (moisture ingress)
   - Wiring fault (break in limit switch loop)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Check limit switch indicator on PLC panel (if available)
   b. Measure continuity across limit switch terminals:
      - NC contacts should show ~0Ω when switch NOT activated
      - If measuring open circuit (OL) when not activated → switch stuck or faulty
   c. Manually actuate the switch (careful – ensure crane is safe first)
   d. Inspect actuator arm position and condition
   e. Check wiring for mechanical damage (cable conduit, connector)

2. HOOK BLOCK OVER-TRAVELS (Limit switch fails to stop motion)
   Causes:
   - Limit switch contacts welded closed (due to arcing)
   - Limit switch not positioned correctly (adjustment needed)
   - Bypass wired (NEVER acceptable in safety circuit)
   - Double-insulated limit switch wired to wrong circuit terminal

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Test switch operation: manually trigger it → hoist must stop in UP direction
   b. Check switch contact resistance when activated: should be open circuit (OL)
   c. Verify wiring polarity and circuit type (NC vs. NO)
   d. Verify limit switch is correctly positioned (hook block must contact actuator at correct height)

3. TRAVEL LIMIT SWITCH FAILURE
   Causes:
   - Actuator/buffer struck too hard (switch housing cracked)
   - Alignment drift over time (vibration)
   - Water ingress to switch housing
   - Cable damage from crane movement

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Visual inspection of switch housing for cracks or displacement
   b. Check actuator alignment with travelling assembly striker plate
   c. Test: drive crane toward limit at slow speed → should stop before mechanical buffer

=== ADJUSTMENT PROCEDURE ===
Upper Hoist Limit:
1. Lock out crane (LOTO procedure)
2. Manually position hook at desired stop height (minimum 300 mm below hoist body)
3. Adjust cam or rotary counter position to activate limit switch at this position
4. Restore power
5. Slowly raise hook to verify limit activates at correct position
6. Confirm: after limit activation, DOWN direction still operable
7. Confirm: UP direction blocked after limit activation
8. Record adjustment in maintenance log

=== INSPECTION SCHEDULE ===
- Before each shift: functional test of upper hoist limit (raise hook until limit activates)
- Monthly: full inspection of all limit switches (continuity, alignment, housing condition)
- Annually: replace any limit switch older than 5 years or showing wear

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter               | Normal (Not Activated) | Normal (Activated)
------------------------|------------------------|--------------------

Activation force        | Per OEM spec          | N/A
Clearance to hoist body | >300 mm at activation | N/A
