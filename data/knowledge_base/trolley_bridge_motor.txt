COMPONENT: Trolley Motor / Bridge Motor (Travel Drives)
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: Overhead Crane Trolley and Bridge Travel Mechanisms

=== OVERVIEW ===
Travel motors drive the horizontal movement of the crane – the trolley moves the load transversely (cross-travel), and the bridge moves longitudinally (long-travel). Both are typically pole-changing asynchronous motors with foot-mounted or flange-mounted construction, driving through a gearbox to drive wheels.

=== TYPICAL SPECIFICATIONS ===
- Motor type: Pole-changing 4/16-pole (2-speed) or VFD-controlled single-speed
- Speeds: 2-speed (slow/fast) or stepless (with VFD)
- Duty cycle: S3-25% to S3-40% (intermittent travel)
- Brake: Integral or gearbox-mounted (same principles as hoist brake)
- Drive wheel diameter: 100–400 mm depending on crane size
- Wheel base and runway gauge: must match girder/runway design

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. CRANE/TROLLEY DOES NOT TRAVEL IN ONE OR BOTH DIRECTIONS
   Causes:
   - Travel limit switch activated (end-of-travel)
   - Direction contactor failure
   - Drive wheel locked (brake not releasing)
   - Motor overload trip

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Check if motion is blocked in both directions or only one
   b. Both blocked: check main motor fuse, overload relay, motor thermal trip
   c. One direction only: check contactor for that direction, travel limit switch
   d. Can wheels be turned by hand? If not → brake issue

2. CRANE SKEWING / DIAGONAL TRAVEL
   Causes (bridge travel only):
   - One end-drive motor failed (single-girder: one motor per end)
   - Drive wheel worn flat on one side
   - Rail misalignment (check runway alignment)
   - Speed controller difference between two end motors

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Observe bridge movement: does it move diagonally?
   b. Check both end-drive motors are running (current on both sides)
   c. Check wheel flanges for wear (flange touching rail = skew)
   d. Measure drive wheel diameter on both ends (wear causes speed difference)

3. TRAVEL SPEED PROBLEMS
   Causes:
   - VFD parameter error or fault
   - Speed selector switch fault (2-speed motor: only one speed available)
   - Brake drag (partial engagement causes speed reduction)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. VFD: check fault display on VFD panel
   b. 2-speed motor: verify correct poles selected (4-pole = fast, 16-pole = slow)
   c. Check brake release confirmation signal

4. DRIVE WHEEL WEAR / RAIL WEAR
   - Inspect wheel flange thickness monthly
   - Wheel flat spots cause impact loading and vibration
   - Rail gauge wear: check rail width at multiple points

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter              | Normal               | Action Threshold
-----------------------|----------------------|------------------
Running Current        | ≤100% FLA            | >110% FLA → Investigate
Travel Speed (full)    | Per design ±5%       | >±10% → Check gearbox/brake
Wheel Flange Thickness | Per OEM min (typ 20mm)| <15 mm → Replace wheel
Rail Wear (width)      | Nominal ±2 mm        | >±4 mm → Replace rail section
Brake operation        | Same as hoist brake  | See hoist_brake.txt
