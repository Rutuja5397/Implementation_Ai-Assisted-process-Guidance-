COMPONENT: Power Supply (Main Supply, Busbar, Cable Reel, Festoon)
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: Overhead Cranes with Fixed or Travelling Power Supply

=== OVERVIEW ===
The crane power supply delivers three-phase (and control voltage) to the crane from a fixed supply point to the travelling crane. Common methods: rigid busbars/conductor rails, cable reel systems, or festoon cable systems. Faults in the power supply cause crane-wide failures, intermittent faults, or voltage drop issues.

=== POWER SUPPLY TYPES ===
1. Conductor Rail (Busbar): Copper or aluminium conductors mounted along runway; sliding collectors on crane
2. Cable Festoon: Cables hung from festoon carriers; moves with bridge/trolley
3. Cable Reel: Spring-wound or motorised drum pays out/rewinds cable as crane travels
4. Busduct (enclosed busbar): Fully enclosed, higher IP rating

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. COMPLETE POWER LOSS (Crane dead)
   Causes:
   - Supply breaker tripped at substation
   - Conductor rail break or gap misalignment
   - Main cable failure (cut, overheated, short circuit)
   - Cable reel internal break

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Check supply at panel incoming terminals: 3-phase voltage present?
   b. Check busbar/conductor rail current collectors for contact condition
   c. Measure voltage at crane panel with crane in different positions along runway
   d. Check cable reel: any cable damage visible at drum entry point?

2. INTERMITTENT POWER (Crane cuts out while travelling)
   Causes:
   - Conductor rail collector spring worn (poor contact)
   - Conductor rail gap or misalignment at joint
   - Festoon cable tangling or snagging
   - Cable reel slip ring fault (arcing at slip ring contact)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Note: Does fault occur at specific position on runway? → Rail gap or joint issue
   b. Does fault occur when crane starts moving? → Collector or festoon issue
   c. Inspect conductor rail collector shoes: wear, spring pressure
   d. Check rail joints: continuity across joints, physical alignment

3. VOLTAGE DROP (Crane operates slowly, motors run hot)
   Causes:
   - Undersized conductors for long span
   - Poor connections (high resistance joints)
   - Corroded busbar surface

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Measure voltage at crane panel input with full load operating
   b. Compare to supply voltage at substation → voltage drop = (V_source - V_crane)
   c. Acceptable voltage drop: maximum 5% of rated voltage under full load
   d. >5% drop → check connections, conductor condition, conductor sizing

4. EARTH FAULT / INSULATION FAILURE
   Causes:
   - Cable insulation aged or damaged
   - Moisture ingress to terminal connections
   - Collector contamination (conductive dust bridging phases or to earth)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Earth fault indicator (if fitted) or RCD/GFCI trip
   b. Insulation resistance test (500V DC megger) on power supply conductors
   c. Minimum acceptable: 1 MΩ phase-to-earth
   d. Isolate sections to identify fault location

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter               | Normal              | Action Threshold
------------------------|---------------------|------------------
Incoming voltage        | 380–415V (±5%)      | <361V or >436V
Voltage drop under load | <5% of rated        | >5% → investigate supply
Phase imbalance         | <2%                 | >3% → investigate supply
Insulation resistance   | >1 MΩ               | <1 MΩ → do not energise
Collector contact force | Per OEM spec        | Reduced → adjust/replace spring
Earth leakage current   | <30 mA (typical RCD)| RCD trips → find fault
