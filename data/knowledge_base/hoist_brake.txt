COMPONENT: Hoist Brake
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: Demag EKKE 5t, Liebherr Tower Crane, Generic Overhead Crane

=== OVERVIEW ===
The hoist brake is a fail-safe electromagnetic disc or shoe brake mounted on the motor shaft. It applies automatically when power is removed (spring-applied, electrically-released principle). The brake must hold 150% of rated load as per EN 14492-2 / FEM standards.

=== TYPES ===
1. DC Electromagnetic Disc Brake (most common on Demag EKKE)
   - Coil voltage: 24VDC (supplied via half-wave or full-wave rectifier from 230VAC)
   - Spring-applied, electromagnet-released
   - Air gap: 0.15–0.4 mm (nominal 0.2–0.3 mm)

2. AC Electromagnetic Shoe Brake
   - Coil voltage: 230VAC or 400VAC
   - Releases when energised

=== ELECTRICAL SPECIFICATIONS ===
- DC brake coil resistance: typically 20–100 Ω (check nameplate)
- Coil voltage tolerance: ±10% of rated voltage
- Release time: <100 ms
- Engagement time (spring): <150 ms
- Rectifier output (no-load): ~24–32 VDC
- Min. holding voltage: 0.7 × rated (for DC types)

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. BRAKE DOES NOT RELEASE (Load does not lift; motor strains)
   Causes:
   - Brake coil no voltage supply
   - Rectifier failure (AC to DC conversion failed)
   - Coil burnt out (open circuit)
   - Air gap too large (exceeds maximum)
   - Brake disc seized/corroded (outdoor use)
   - Control relay or contactor not energising brake circuit

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Measure voltage at brake coil terminals:
      - With motor running: should read rated DC voltage (e.g., 24VDC ±10%)
      - If 0V → check rectifier input, fuse, and control circuit
   b. Measure rectifier output with motor running
      - AC input to rectifier: 230VAC ±10%
      - DC output: ~24–32 VDC (check polarity)
   c. Measure brake coil resistance (motor OFF, disconnected):
      - Open circuit → coil burnt, replace brake
      - Short circuit (<5Ω for 24V coil) → coil shorted, replace brake
   d. Measure air gap with feeler gauge at 3 points around disc
      - If gap > 0.5 mm → adjust or replace brake disc
   e. Manual release test: engage manual brake release lever (if fitted)
      - If load lifts when manually released → electrical/control issue confirmed

2. BRAKE DOES NOT ENGAGE (Load drifts when stopped – SAFETY CRITICAL)
   Causes:
   - Spring broken or fatigued (reduced spring force)
   - Brake disc worn below minimum thickness
   - Oil or grease contamination of brake disc/pad
   - Air gap too small (disc drag during operation)
   - Coil not de-energising (stuck contactor)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Load drift test: slowly lower rated load, release pendant – does load stop?
   b. Measure brake coil voltage with motor off:
      - Should be 0V → if voltage remains, contactor is stuck closed
   c. Check disc thickness (caliper measurement):
      - Minimum disc thickness: typically 6–8 mm (check OEM data)
      - Minimum pad thickness: 3 mm
   d. Check for oil contamination (visual on disc friction surface)
   e. Static holding test: hang 150% rated load, brake engaged – must hold

3. BRAKE OVERHEATING / BURNING SMELL
   Causes:
   - Brake dragging (partial release, air gap too small)
   - Excessive duty cycle
   - Coil overvoltage

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Check coil voltage does not exceed 110% rated
   b. Measure air gap – if <0.1 mm, disc rubbing continuously
   c. Check surface temperature of brake housing (normal <80°C in operation)

4. ABNORMAL NOISE (Clicking, chattering, grinding)
   Causes:
   - Worn armature plate (chattering = AC hum)
   - Loose brake mounting
   - Disc runout excessive (grinding)

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter                | Normal Range         | Action Threshold
-------------------------|---------------------|------------------
Brake Coil Voltage (DC)  | 21.6–26.4V (24V nom)| <20V → Adjust/check rectifier
Coil Resistance          | Per OEM spec ±10%   | Open/short → Replace immediately
Air Gap                  | 0.2–0.3 mm          | >0.45 mm → Adjust; >0.5 mm → Replace
Disc Thickness           | >6 mm (OEM min)     | <4 mm → Replace disc
Brake Housing Temp       | <80°C               | >100°C → Stop, investigate drag
Static Hold (% rated)    | 150% load held      | Any drift → STOP, DO NOT USE

=== ADJUSTMENT PROCEDURE (Air Gap) ===
1. De-energise entire crane (LOTO)
2. Remove protective cover
3. Measure air gap at 3 points (120° apart) with feeler gauge
4. Adjust via brake adjustment nuts/screws (OEM-specific)
5. Target: 0.2–0.3 mm uniform gap
6. Manually actuate brake several times to verify smooth operation
7. Restore power, perform no-load test

=== DEMAG EKKE 5t SPECIFIC NOTES ===
- Brake type: KFB integrated electromagnetic disc brake
- Nominal torque: 6–16 Nm (dependent on motor size)
- Coil: 24VDC via full-wave rectifier module (mounted in control box)
- Air gap nominal: 0.2 mm; Max: 0.4 mm
- Replacement interval: disc replacement at >0.5 mm gap or visible scoring
