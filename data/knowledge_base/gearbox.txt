COMPONENT: Gearbox (Hoist / Trolley / Bridge)
DOCUMENT TYPE: Maintenance and Troubleshooting Manual
APPLIES TO: Overhead Cranes, Hoist Units, Crane Travel Drives

=== OVERVIEW ===
The gearbox reduces motor speed and multiplies torque to drive the hoist drum or travel wheels. Hoist gearboxes are typically helical or bevel-helical type, enclosed in a cast iron or welded steel housing with splash or forced lubrication.

=== KEY PARAMETERS ===
- Gear ratio: 10:1 to 200:1 (application dependent)
- Lubrication: splash oil bath (small units) or forced circulation (large units)
- Oil grade: ISO VG 220 or ISO VG 320 (most hoist gearboxes); check nameplate
- Operating temperature: max 80°C oil temperature
- Oil volume: check sight glass or dipstick; do not overfill (>75% of sight glass = overfull)

=== COMMON FAILURE MODES ===

1. ABNORMAL NOISE – GRINDING
   Causes:
   - Gear tooth damage (pitting, spalling, or breakage)
   - Bearing damage (brinelling, race damage)
   - Contamination in oil (metal particles)
   - Insufficient lubrication

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Listen carefully: grinding with load = gear damage; continuous grinding = bearing
   b. Check oil level: low oil → inadequate lubrication
   c. Take oil sample: metal particles, dark colour, milky appearance (water ingress)
   d. Check oil temperature via IR thermometer at gearbox housing
   e. If metal particles found → drain oil, inspect gears, replace bearings

2. ABNORMAL NOISE – WHINING
   Causes:
   - Gear misalignment (worn shafts, bearing wear)
   - Wrong oil viscosity (too thin)
   - Early-stage gear pitting

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Whining increases with load speed → gear alignment issue
   b. Whining constant at all loads → oil viscosity issue
   c. Check oil viscosity grade vs. nameplate specification

3. OIL LEAKAGE
   Causes:
   - Worn shaft seals (lip seals at input/output shafts)
   - Gasket failure (cover plate gasket)
   - Overfilled oil (excessive splash → leaks past seals)
   - Housing crack (rare; impact damage)

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Identify leak point: input shaft, output shaft, drain plug, breather, or cover
   b. Check oil level: overfill is common cause
   c. Inspect breather/vent: blocked breather → pressure buildup → seal leakage
   d. Clean exterior, run briefly, identify active leak point

4. GEARBOX OVERHEATING (Oil temp >80°C)
   Causes:
   - Low oil level (inadequate cooling)
   - Wrong oil viscosity (too thick = excessive churning heat)
   - Overloaded crane
   - Blocked oil cooler (forced lubrication systems)
   - Bearing failure causing heat generation

   Diagnostic steps:
   a. Measure oil temperature (IR gun on housing, or temperature probe in oil)
   b. Check oil level and viscosity grade
   c. Verify crane is not overloaded (check load cell if fitted)
   d. Allow gearbox to cool; restart with monitoring

5. VIBRATION / SHAFT MOVEMENT
   Causes:
   - Worn gearbox mounting (anti-vibration mounts)
   - Output shaft bearing failure
   - Gear tooth breakage (sudden onset vibration)

=== OIL ANALYSIS INTERPRETATION ===
Normal oil: amber/golden colour, clean, no particles
Warning signs:
- Dark brown/black → oxidation, overheating history
- Milky/cloudy → water contamination (seal failure or condensation)
- Grey metallic particles → gear/bearing wear (URGENT)
- Sludge at bottom → severe degradation

Oil change intervals:
- Initial fill: change at 500 operating hours or 3 months (whichever first) to flush break-in wear particles
- Standard interval: 2000 hours or annually
- Severe conditions (dusty, hot): 1000 hours

=== MEASUREMENT REFERENCE TABLE ===
Parameter              | Normal Range          | Action Threshold
-----------------------|-----------------------|------------------
Oil Temperature        | 40–75°C               | >80°C → Reduce duty; investigate
Oil Level              | Mid-sight-glass        | Below min → Stop; replenish
Oil Colour             | Amber/clean           | Grey/milky → Change immediately
Gearbox Vibration      | <2.8 mm/s RMS         | >4.5 mm/s RMS → Inspect gears
Noise Level (dB)       | Baseline ±3 dB        | >10 dB increase → Investigate

=== OIL GRADES BY APPLICATION ===
Application            | Recommended Grade
-----------------------|------------------
Hoist gearbox          | ISO VG 220 (mineral) or PAO 220 (synthetic)
Trolley travel         | ISO VG 150 or VG 220
Bridge travel          | ISO VG 220
High temp. environment | ISO VG 320 or synthetic equivalent
